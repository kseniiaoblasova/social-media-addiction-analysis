# Social Media Addiction — Exploratory Data Analysis

In this project, I perform an exploratory data analysis of the *Students' Social Media Addiction* dataset sourced from Kaggle.

The dataset contains survey responses from students across the globe, capturing their social media usage habits and self-reported impact on various aspects of their lives. Through visualizations and correlation analysis, I explore how daily usage influences sleep duration, academic performance, mental health, interpersonal relationships, and addiction scores.

---

## Dataset Overview

**Scope & Coverage**
- **Population:** Students aged 16–25 enrolled in high school, undergraduate, or graduate programs
- **Geography:** Multi-country coverage including Bangladesh, India, USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, Brazil, Japan, and South Korea
- **Timeframe:** One-time online survey administered in Q1 2025

**Limitations**
- **Self-Report Bias:** All measures are self-reported and may be subject to social-desirability effects
- **Cross-Sectional Design:** One-time survey design prevents causal inference
- **Sampling Variability:** Online recruitment may underrepresent students with limited internet access
